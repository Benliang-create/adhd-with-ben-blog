## Tool 1: The ADHD Brain Dump Worksheet

You know that feeling when your brain is running at 1000mph and you can't grab a single thought long enough to do anything with it? That's where the brain dump comes in.

A brain dump is exactly what it sounds like: you dump everything in your head onto paper. No organizing, no prioritizing, no judgment. Just... get it out.

But a blank page can be paralyzing for ADHD brains (ironic, right?). That's why I created a structured [ADHD Brain Dump Worksheet](https://adhdwithben.gumroad.com/l/adhd-brain-dump) that guides the dump without restricting it.

### How It Works

**Section 1: The Dump Zone.** Write everything. Tasks, worries, random ideas, that thing you remembered from 2019, the song stuck in your head â€” all of it. Fill the space. Don't stop to think about whether it "belongs."

**Section 2: The Worry Dump.** Separate out the anxious thoughts. Things you're dreading, worst-case scenarios playing on loop, social situations you're replaying. Getting these out of the main dump helps you see that your "to-do list" is actually 30% tasks and 70% anxiety.

**Section 3: The Priority Sort.** Now that everything's on paper, circle the 3 things that actually need to happen today. Not the 15 things you feel like you "should" do. Three.

**Section 4: The Next Action.** For each of those 3 priorities, write the tiniest possible next step. Not "finish the project" â€” more like "open the document." ADHD brains need activation energy to be as low as possible.

### When I Use It

- Every morning before I even look at my [daily planner](https://adhdwithben.gumroad.com/l/adhd-planner)
- When I'm spiraling and can't focus
- Before bed when my brain won't shut up
- Any time I feel overwhelmed and can't name why

I print stacks of these. They're disposable by design â€” use it, extract your tasks, recycle the paper. No precious journal to maintain.
