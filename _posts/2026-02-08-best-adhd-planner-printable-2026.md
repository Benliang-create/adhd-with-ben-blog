## Why Most Planners Fail People with ADHD

Before I get into the specific planners, let's talk about why the standard advice doesn't work:

**Too much structure kills motivation.** Hour-by-hour schedules look great on paper. But when you miss your 9 AM slot because you were hyperfocused on something else, the whole day feels "ruined." ADHD brains need flexible structure â€” guardrails, not cages.

**Streak-based tracking creates shame spirals.** Miss one day on your habit tracker? That beautiful chain is broken, and your brain says "why bother." We need progress tracking that celebrates doing something, not punishes inconsistency.

**No room for the mental chaos.** ADHD means having 47 thoughts at once. If your planner doesn't give you somewhere to dump all of that, you'll never get to the actual planning part.

## Tips for Actually Sticking with an ADHD Planner

1. **Print multiples.** If you lose one, grab another. Reduce friction.

4. **Forgive the gaps.** Skipped a week? Cool. Start fresh today.
5. **Keep it visible.** Digital planners get buried. Paper on your desk gets noticed.
